Consider which academic pathway/ experiences you wish to pursue early.

  • UG QFIN is not quant enough: it is a delicate balance between Finance, Computer Science (COSC) and Mathematics (MATH). Hence, many senior students (~1/3) take a MATH or COSC double major.
  • In general the double major will lie in one of the three categories. I asked students pursuing each for advice, but decided not to engage further.
    1. COSC
    2. MATH/ MAEC
    3. Other business school majors
  • Taking triple majors are possible but super heavy load.
    • We have two students (2022) taking this path. I know that in the year 2025, more people have achieved so by taking many shortcuts.
    • DDP's Aaron Wang has written a Triple Major Guide for Dualers; worth referencing.

Career Basics

You're in a business school after all - expand your network and polish yourself well.

  • QFIN CMP runs for Y2 and 3, where executive-level business leaders would be our mentors.
  • Peer mentors relate to your struggles more, and could give you more advice being in HKUST.
    And for seniors, a way to contribute back to the community while knowing more future bright talents.
  • Linkedin: Be proactive but not overly aggressive.
  • School offers professional portrait shoots. Check your emails!

You need a decent CV to get past job recruitment screenings.

  • Know which style (Traditional | Skill-based | Academic) to use for various purposes.
    CV Samples on the right are all for traditional CVs.
  • Recruiters on average spend 7 seconds on scanning your CV. You should consider the following:
    • One page rule for traditional CV, esp. for banks; Best 10-11 pt font size (400-550 words).
      See Enhancv and Indeed for CV length guidance.
    • Education: Advised to only include uni/ exchange in Y3-4 with more work exp
      Could include sec school/ relevant uni courses to highlight your excellence/ industry interest.
    • Quantify achievements and work impact in numbers within bullets.
    • Keep bullets short (1-2 lines each), Feature all important info on the left ONLY.
    • Use more action verbs
  • Depending your CV progress, below is a suggestion of you visiting each of the resource:
    1. Kickstarting: LSE's CV Intro offers a quick guidance/ familiarization on what to include.
    2. Reference: Oxford CV Sample 1 (for freshmen); LSE CV sample (for senior students).
    3. Refining/consolidating: Action verbs from Harvard (P.4)/ Stanford guidebooks (P.3) would help.

Kickstarting a successful career takes dedication, awareness and planning.
